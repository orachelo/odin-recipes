Kenyan Githeri

Description

Githeri is a Kenyan traditional meal of maize and legumes, mostly beans of any type, mixed and boiled together. It can be prepared with any type of beans and eaten right after boiling or fried as below.

It is high in carbohydrates & fibre, low in fat & cholesterol and protein is in the beans.

Ingredients

  • 4 medium sized potatoes
  • Vegetable oil
  • 1 medium-sized onion
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon of chilli ground
  • 2 cups of beans
  • 1 cup of maize
  • 2 large chopped tomatoes
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander

Steps

  1. Mix the maize and beans ratio 1:2 then boil till soft.
  2. Add peeled potatoes to a pan, and boil slightly.
  3. Drain all the water in both pans and then set aside.
  4. Heat oil in another pan in medium heat.
  5. Add onions and fry until golden brown.
  6. Add your chopped tomatoes and continue stirring.
  7. Add the boiled beans and maize mixture and stir well.
  8. Pour in the paprika while stirring for another minute to prevent burning
  9. Add 1 cup of water,salt, boiled potatoes, then cover the pan and allow to cook for about 20 minutes.
  10. Sprinkle your chopped coriander leaves, mix well, and serve.
